### **1. Exponential Smoothing Models**
- **Simple Exponential Smoothing (SES)**: For non-trending and non-seasonal data.
- **Holt’s Linear Trend Method**: To handle a linear trend without seasonality.
- **Holt-Winters Methods**:
  - **Additive Seasonal Holt-Winters**: For linear trends with additive seasonality.
  - **Multiplicative Seasonal Holt-Winters**: For linear trends with multiplicative seasonality.
- Damped Seasonal Holt-Winters Model: Test with dampened trends and seasonal effects.

### **2. ARIMA Models**
- **Non-Seasonal ARIMA (AutoRegressive Integrated Moving Average)**:
  - Includes models like ARIMA(0,1,0) (random walk) and ARIMA(0,2,1) (linear exponential smoothing).
- **Seasonal ARIMA (SARIMA)**:
  - Accounts for both seasonal and non-seasonal components (e.g., SARIMA(p, d, q)(P, D, Q)[T]).

### **3. Regression Models**
- **Linear Regression**: Useful as a baseline but may not be efficient if the data contains trends or seasonality.
- **Regression with Time Components**: To account for periodic effects or external predictors (e.g., temperature in your case).

### **4. Model Selection and Evaluation**
For each model:
- **Parameter Tuning**: Adjust parameters (e.g., α, β, γ for exponential smoothing; p, d, q for ARIMA).

- **Performance Metrics**: Use RMSE, MAPE, or other suitable error metrics.

### **Additional Techniques Covered**
- **Moving Averages**: For trend or seasonal component smoothing.
